The inbox is where Multica **interrupts** you — [tasks](/issues) assigned to you, [`@` mentions](/comments), and activity on tasks you're subscribed to all land here.

You control which task activity reaches you by **subscribing** and **unsubscribing**.

## What shows up in your inbox

The following events deliver a notification to your inbox:

- **Task assigned / unassigned / reassigned** — you're notified when you're the new (or former) assignee
- **Status, priority, or due date change on a task you're subscribed to**
- **New comment on a task you're subscribed to**
- **You're `@`-mentioned in a comment** — delivered whether or not you're subscribed
- **Someone reacts to your task or comment**
- **A digital employee [task](/tasks) you assigned fails**

## `@all` notifies the entire workspace

`@all` is a special target — it pushes a notification to **every member** of the workspace.

<Callout type="warning">
**Use `@all` sparingly.** In a 50-person workspace, one `@all` comment produces 50 inbox notifications instantly. Reserve it for high-stakes events (production incidents, milestone announcements) — not everyday discussion.
</Callout>

## Digital employees never receive notifications

Digital employees **never** get inbox notifications — not even when they're the assignee, creator, or `@`-mentioned in a comment.

This isn't a bug: digital employees don't read an inbox. They work by [**immediate trigger**](/assigning-issues) — assigning a task or `@`-mentioning the digital employee in a comment kicks off a task for it right away. The inbox is a reminder mechanism for humans; it has no meaning for digital employees.

## Subscription rules

You're **auto-subscribed** to a task in four situations:

- You **created** it
- You were **assigned** to it
- You **commented** on it
- You were **`@`-mentioned** on it or in one of its comments

Auto-subscription happens once — being both the creator and a mentionee doesn't subscribe you twice.

<Callout type="warning">
**Reassignment doesn't auto-unsubscribe you.** If you used to be the assignee and got replaced, you'll **still receive updates on that task** — the auto-subscription stays in the database.

To stop getting notified, open the task and unsubscribe manually.
</Callout>

You can also **manually subscribe** to any task (even unrelated ones), or **manually unsubscribe** from any auto-subscription. In the UI, use the right panel on the task page; in the CLI, use `multica issue subscriber add/remove`.

## Sub-task status changes bubble up to the parent

When a sub-task's **status** changes, subscribers of the parent task are notified too — even if they haven't subscribed to the sub-task.

This applies to **status only**: comment, priority, and due date changes on sub-tasks do **not** bubble up.

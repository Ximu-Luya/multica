A digital department is a **named group of [digital employees](/agents) and human [members](/members-roles)**, with one designated **leader digital employee**. The digital department is itself a first-class assignee: pick it from any **Assignee** picker and the leader takes the trigger, reads the task, then `@`-mentions the digital department member best suited to do the work. Digital departments let you assemble specialists once and dispatch them **by topic instead of by name** — the team grows, the routing stays the same.

## What a digital department is, in mechanics

- **One leader, many members.** The leader must be a digital employee; members can be digital employees or human members. A digital department with only the leader is allowed (the leader briefing notes "no other members"), and the same digital employee can sit in multiple digital departments.
- **Assignable everywhere a person is.** Digital departments appear in the Assignee picker, the @-mention picker, and the quick-create modal — anywhere you'd pick a digital employee or member, you can pick a digital department.
- **Soft-deleted via archive.** Archive a digital department and it disappears from pickers and lists; any task currently assigned to it is **transferred to the leader digital employee** so the work doesn't go silent. Archived digital departments can't be assigned to new tasks.

## When to use a digital department versus a single digital employee

| Pick a digital department when… | Pick a single digital employee when… |
|---|---|
| You have several specialists and don't know which one fits this task in advance | The work is well-scoped to one specialty and you know who should do it |
| You want one stable assignee (the digital department) while the actual responder changes per task | You want the digital employee's name on the task and clear individual accountability |
| You want a `@FrontendTeam` style routing target in comments | One-on-one `@agent-name` is enough |

The digital department doesn't add capability — it adds **routing**. The members are still ordinary digital employees; the leader's only job is to pick the right one.

## Permissions

| Action | Who can do it |
|---|---|
| Create / update / archive a digital department | Workspace **owner** or **admin** |
| Add or remove members, change roles | Workspace **owner** or **admin** |
| Assign a task to a digital department | Any workspace member (same as assigning to a digital employee) |
| `@`-mention a digital department in a comment | Any workspace member |
| Record a digital department leader evaluation | The digital department leader digital employee only (via CLI) |

The full role matrix lives in [Members and roles](/members-roles).

## Create a digital department

In the sidebar, open **Digital departments → New digital department** and fill in:

- **Name** — e.g. `Frontend Team`, `Bug Triage`. Doesn't need to be unique within the workspace.
- **Description** (optional) — a short blurb shown on the digital department card and detail page.
- **Leader** — pick an existing digital employee. The leader is added to the digital department automatically with role `leader`.

After creation, open the digital department's detail page to:

- **Add members** — pick digital employees or human members, optionally give each a short role description (e.g. "owns the migrations", "reviewer of last resort"). The leader uses these roles when deciding who to delegate to.
- **Write instructions** — digital department-level guidance the leader sees on every run (more below).
- **Set an avatar** — picked from the same picker used for digital employees.

CLI equivalent:

```bash
multica squad create --name "Frontend Team" --leader frontend-lead-agent
multica squad member add <squad-id> --member-id <agent-or-user-uuid> --type agent --role "Owns Tailwind / shadcn surface"
```

## How a digital department-assigned task runs

When a non-Backlog task is assigned to a digital department, Multica immediately enqueues a `task` for the **leader digital employee** (not for every member). The flow then looks like this:

1. **Leader claims the task.** The digital employee runtime picks up the task on its next poll, same as any other digital employee assignment.
2. **Leader is briefed.** On claim, Multica appends three sections to the leader's system prompt — see [What the leader sees on every turn](#what-the-leader-sees-on-every-turn) below.
3. **Leader posts one delegation comment.** The comment `@`-mentions the chosen member(s) using the exact mention markdown from the roster — that mention triggers a new `task` for each mentioned digital employee.
4. **Leader records its evaluation** via `multica squad activity <issue-id> action --reason "..."`. This writes an entry to the task's activity timeline so humans can see the leader actually evaluated the trigger.
5. **Leader stops.** The leader does not do the implementation itself. When the delegated member posts back, the leader is re-triggered to read the update and either delegate the next step, escalate, or stay silent.

If the task is in **Backlog**, the leader is not triggered — Backlog is a parking lot, same rule as for direct digital employee assignment.

### What the leader sees on every turn

On each digital department-leader run, three blocks are appended to the leader's instructions:

- **Digital Department Operating Protocol** — a hard-coded rule set: read the task, delegate by `@`-mention, be terse (don't restate the task body — the assignee can read it), record an evaluation every turn, and **stop after dispatching**. This protocol is system-managed and not editable.
- **Digital Department Roster** — the leader's self-row plus one row per non-archived member. Each row carries the exact mention markdown (`[@Name](mention://agent/<uuid>)` or `[@Name](mention://member/<uuid>)`) the leader should paste — typing a plain `@name` won't trigger anyone.
- **Digital Department Instructions** — your custom guidance for this digital department (set on the digital department detail page or via `multica squad update --instructions`). Use this for routing rules ("send DB work to Alice, frontend to Bob"), escalation policies, or anything else the leader needs to know that isn't already in the task.

## When the leader is re-triggered

After the first dispatch, the leader is woken up automatically by **most subsequent comments** on the task. The exact rules:

| Event | Leader triggered? |
|---|---|
| A non-member (human reporter, external digital employee) posts a comment | **Yes** |
| A digital department member posts a progress update with no `@mention` | **Yes** — the leader re-evaluates whether the next step is needed |
| Anyone posts a comment that explicitly `@`-mentions another digital employee / member / digital department / `@all` | **No** — the explicit `@` is the routing signal; the leader gets out of the way |
| The leader's own comment (self-trigger) | **No** — guarded to prevent a loop |
| A comment containing only a task cross-reference (`[MUL-123](mention://issue/...)`) | **Yes** — task references aren't routing |

Dedup applies on top of these rules: if the leader already has a `queued` or `dispatched` task on this task, a new trigger won't enqueue a duplicate.

<Callout type="info">
**Why the leader doesn't trigger when a member posts an `@`-mention.** Once a digital department member directly `@`s someone, that comment is a deliberate hand-off — having the leader wake up to "observe" the routing would just produce a no-op turn and clutter the timeline. Digital employee-authored comments are the exception: when a digital employee posts a result that `@`s another digital employee, the leader still wakes up so it can coordinate the thread.
</Callout>

## `@`-mention a digital department in a comment

Digital departments appear in the `@` picker alongside members and digital employees. Mentioning a digital department inserts `[@SquadName](mention://squad/<uuid>)` and triggers the **digital department leader** as if you had assigned the task to the digital department — without changing the assignee or the status. Use this when you want the digital department to pick someone for a question or sub-task while keeping the current owner.

The same anti-loop rules apply: the leader skips itself, and an explicit member `@`-mention in the same comment will route to that member directly.

## Reassign or archive a digital department

**Reassigning a task away from a digital department** behaves like any other assignee change: all of the task's active tasks (including the leader's) are cancelled, and the new assignee — digital employee, member, or another digital department — is enqueued. There is no separate "remove digital department without changing assignee" action; pick a different assignee.

**Archiving a digital department** (`multica squad delete <id>`, or the Archive button on the detail page):

1. **Transfers tasks currently assigned to the digital department to the leader digital employee**, so the work continues against a concrete digital employee instead of going silent.
2. Marks the digital department with `archived_at` / `archived_by` — the row is preserved so historical activity entries still resolve, but the digital department disappears from lists, pickers, and the @-mention dropdown.
3. **Rejects future assignments** to this digital department with `cannot assign to an archived digital department`.

There is currently no unarchive command; create a new digital department if you need the routing back.

## Digital department operations from the CLI

| Command | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `multica squad list` | List digital departments in the workspace |
| `multica squad get <id>` | Show one digital department's name, leader, description, instructions |
| `multica squad create --name "..." --leader <agent>` | Create a digital department (owner / admin) |
| `multica squad update <id> [--name X] [--description X] [--instructions X] [--leader Y] [--avatar-url Z]` | Update one or more fields |
| `multica squad delete <id>` | Archive (soft-delete) — transfers assigned tasks to the leader |
| `multica squad member list <id>` | List a digital department's members |
| `multica squad member add <id> --member-id <uuid> --type agent\|member [--role "..."]` | Add a member (owner / admin) |
| `multica squad member remove <id> --member-id <uuid> --type agent\|member` | Remove a member (the leader cannot be removed — change leader first) |
| `multica squad activity <issue-id> <action\|no_action\|failed> --reason "..."` | Recorded by the leader digital employee at the end of every turn |

`--leader` accepts a digital employee name or UUID; for everything else, IDs come from `multica agent list --output json`, `multica workspace member list --output json`, and `multica squad list --output json`.

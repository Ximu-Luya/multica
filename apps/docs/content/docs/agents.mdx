A digital employee is a **first-class member** of a Multica [workspace](/workspaces) — like a human, it can be [assigned tasks](/assigning-issues), speak up in [comments](/comments), be [`@`-mentioned](/mentioning-agents), and lead a [project](/projects). The core difference: behind every digital employee is an [AI coding tool](/providers) running on your machine. Assign it a task and it **starts working within seconds** on its own — no nudging, no going offline, available 24/7.

## What a digital employee can do

Digital employees use the same "member" surface as humans, and the UI barely distinguishes them:

- **[Be assigned tasks](/assigning-issues)** — once set as the assignee, it starts working automatically
- **[Be `@`-mentioned](/mentioning-agents)** — write `@agent-name` in a comment and it wakes up to read that comment
- **Post [comments](/comments)** — it reports progress and replies to people under the task
- **Lead a [project](/projects)** — it can be set as project lead, same as a human
- **Open [tasks](/issues) itself** — while running a task, if it spots a related problem, it can create a new task directly

From the collaboration view, a digital employee is just a member of the workspace — its name sits in the same member list as humans, usually with a small robot icon in front.

## How it differs from a human

A few key differences only surface once you actually start using digital employees:

- **It starts on its own** — after you assign it a task or `@` it, Multica dispatches the task to its runtime immediately. Unlike a human, it doesn't wait to see the message and respond. For trigger details, see [Assigning tasks to digital employees](/assigning-issues) and [@-mentioning digital employees in comments](/mentioning-agents).
- **It doesn't receive notifications** — a digital employee never shows up on the other side of your [inbox](/inbox), and it's not in the audience for `@all`. It isn't a "recipient who reads messages" — it's a "work unit that gets triggered to execute tasks."
- **It's bound to one AI coding tool** — every digital employee is tied to a runtime (runtime = daemon × one AI coding tool; see [Daemon and runtimes](/daemon-runtimes)). If the tool is offline, the digital employee can't work; new tasks wait until the runtime comes back.
- **It can be archived** — archive a digital employee you don't use anymore and it disappears from everyday views; restore it whenever you want. Archiving cancels any tasks currently running.

## Who can assign a digital employee

When you create a digital employee, you pick a **visibility** that controls who can assign it to a task or set it as project lead:

- **Workspace** — any member of the workspace can assign it
- **Private** — only workspace owners, admins, or the digital employee's creator can assign it

New digital employees default to **private**. To make one available to the whole workspace, set visibility to `workspace` at creation time, or change it later in the digital employee's config. For the full role-permission matrix, see [Members and roles](/members-roles).

<Callout type="info">
**Private means "restricted who can assign," not "hidden from everyone else."** Every member of the workspace sees a private digital employee's name and description in the digital employees list — they just can't see its config details (custom environment variables, MCP config, and other sensitive fields are masked). If you need "visible to only one person," that's not currently possible.
</Callout>
